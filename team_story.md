# Team Story – SARA AI

SARA AI (Smart Accessible Reader & Assistant) was created by Aditya Rai and Sparsh Ruhela, students of Shaheed Rajpal DAV Public School, New Delhi.

The idea for SARA AI came from a simple but powerful observation. Many modern AI learning tools are designed for students who can easily read text, navigate complex interfaces, and interact visually with technology. However, millions of students around the world live with visual impairments or dyslexia. For these students, most educational technology tools are difficult or impossible to use.

During research and discussions with teachers and special education coordinators, we realised that visually impaired students often rely heavily on human assistance to access digital learning materials. We wondered whether artificial intelligence could make this process more independent and empowering.

This led to the creation of SARA AI — a voice-driven AI study assistant that allows students to learn, ask questions, take quizzes, and manage their studies entirely through speech.

Unlike traditional AI chat tools, SARA AI was designed with accessibility as the primary focus. The system supports voice interaction, dyslexia-friendly text modes, multilingual learning, and tools such as syllabus scanning and timetable reading using OCR technology.

One of the most rewarding parts of the project was testing the system with visually impaired students and gathering feedback from teachers. Their suggestions helped improve the usability and accessibility of the platform.

Our goal with SARA AI is not just to build a school project, but to explore how artificial intelligence can make education more inclusive. Technology should empower every student, regardless of physical or learning challenges.

We hope that SARA AI demonstrates how AI can be used not only for innovation, but also for compassion and accessibility in education.
